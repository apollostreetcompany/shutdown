## 1. MARKET SIZE (TAM / SAM / SOM)

### How Many Businesses Close Each Year?

The US sees approximately **1.1 to 1.2 million business establishment deaths per year**, based on Bureau of Labor Statistics Business Employment Dynamics data:

- **Q1 2023:** 291,000 establishment deaths
- **Q2 2023:** 293,000 establishment deaths
- **Q3 2023:** 295,000 establishment deaths
- **Q4 2023:** 322,000 establishment deaths
- **2023 total (est.):** ~1.2 million establishment closures
- **Q1 2024:** 282,000 establishment deaths (later quarters not yet finalized by BLS)

Additionally, Census Bureau data shows **1.13 million businesses closed** between March 2023 and March 2024, with small businesses accounting for **982,940** of those closings.

**Sources:** [BLS Business Employment Dynamics](https://www.bls.gov/bdm/bdmage.htm), [Census Bureau BFS](https://www.census.gov/econ/bfs/index.html)

### Business Failure Rates

Per the Bureau of Labor Statistics:
- **20.4%** of businesses fail in their first year
- **49.4%** fail within their first 5 years
- **65.3%** fail within their first 10 years

**Source:** [Commerce Institute - Business Failure Rate](https://www.commerceinstitute.com/business-failure-rate/)

### LLC vs. Corporation Breakdown

LLCs are the dominant business structure in the US:
- **LLCs:** ~43% of all small businesses (~21.6 million active LLCs as of 2024)
- **S Corporations:** ~30%
- **C Corporations:** ~18%
- **Sole Proprietorships:** ~6%
- **Partnerships:** ~3%

LLCs represent **85% of all new entity formations** as of 2023, meaning the proportion of LLCs among closing businesses will continue to increase over time.

**Sources:** [SBA Small Business Profile 2025](https://advocacy.sba.gov/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/United_States_2025-State-Profile.pdf), [Small Biz Statistics](https://smallbizstatistics.com/statistics/how-many-llcs-formed-per-year/)

## 2. GROWTH RATE AND TRENDS

### Business Formation is Surging (More Formations = More Future Closures)

New business applications have hit record levels and continue to accelerate:

| Year | Business Applications | YoY Change |
|---|---|---|
| 2019 | 3.5 million | baseline |
| 2020 | 4.4 million | +24% (pandemic spike) |
| 2021 | 5.4 million | +22.7% |
| 2022 | 5.1 million | -5% |
| 2023 | 5.5 million | +8% (record) |
| 2024 | 5.48 million | ~flat |
| 2025 | 5.62 million | +8.2% |
| 2026 (Jan-Feb) | 1.03 million | +25.5% vs. prior year period |

**Key insight:** The 2020-2021 formation boom means a corresponding **wave of closures is coming in 2025-2028**, since ~20% of businesses fail in Year 1 and ~50% by Year 5. The 2020-2021 cohort alone represents ~10 million new business applications, of which roughly **5 million will eventually close**.

**Sources:** [Census Bureau BFS](https://www.census.gov/econ/bfs/index.html), [Commerce Institute](https://www.commerceinstitute.com/new-businesses-started-every-year/), [Finder.com](https://www.finder.com/small-business/new-businesses)

### COVID/Post-COVID Trends

- Active business owners plummeted from **15.0 million (Feb 2020) to 11.7 million (April 2020)** before partially rebounding.
- By September 2021, **85% of small businesses** reported full reopening.
- Small businesses were disproportionately impacted vs. large businesses.
- The pandemic triggered a **record entrepreneurship wave** -- many of those pandemic-era businesses are now reaching the critical 3-5 year failure window.

**Source:** [Federal Reserve FEDS Notes](https://www.federalreserve.gov/econres/notes/feds-notes/business-entry-and-exit-in-the-covid-19-pandemic-a-preliminary-look-at-official-data-20220506.html)

### AI Disruption in Legal Services

- Corporate legal AI adoption **more than doubled** from 23% (2024) to 54% (2025).
- Legal aid professionals are adopting AI at **2x the rate** of the broader legal industry.
- The emphasis is shifting to **"augmentation over replacement"** -- AI handles routine tasks while humans oversee complex decisions.
- **Clients are increasingly using AI instead of contacting external law firms** -- this is a direct tailwind for AI-powered dissolution tools.
- The global online legal services market was **$11.53 billion in 2024**, projected to grow to **$36 billion by 2034** (12% CAGR).

**Sources:** [Bloomberg Law 2026](https://pro.bloomberglaw.com/insights/technology/bloomberg-law-2026-key-legal-ai-trends/), [ABA Legal Industry Report 2025](https://www.americanbar.org/groups/law_practice/resources/law-technology-today/2025/the-legal-industry-report-2025/)

### DIY Legal Trend

The trend toward self-service legal tools is strong and accelerating. LegalZoom's subscription model grew 10% YoY to $120M in Q3 2025, indicating consumers are comfortable paying for ongoing legal tech services rather than hiring attorneys for routine matters.

### Is There a Market for a $49 Guide?

**Yes, with caveats.** The data supports a digital guide/course product at this price point:

- ~300,000+ solo entrepreneurs close simple LLCs annually
- Many currently use free but scattered/incomplete resources (IRS.gov, state websites, blog posts)
- A comprehensive, state-specific guide fills the gap between "free but confusing" and "$99+ filing service"
- At $49 with even 1% conversion of the target segment: **$49 x 3,000 = $147K/year** from the guide alone
- Better positioning: **$49-$79 as a lead magnet / entry product** that upsells to a $199-$499 service tier

### Is $1,000 Competitive for Full-Service?

**Yes -- this is the sweet spot gap in the market.**

- Traditional attorneys charge $500-$5,000+ but are slow and opaque on pricing.
- Online services top out at $149 for basic filing only.
- SimpleClosure charges $2,500+ but targets funded startups.
- **A $999 "Complete Dissolution Package"** (filing + tax checklist + compliance verification + multi-state coordination) has no direct online competitor and sits squarely in the underserved middle.

## 6. OPPORTUNITY ANALYSIS

### What's Underserved?

1. **The "Guided Middle" ($200-$1,000):** The market bifurcates between cheap filing ($99-$149) and expensive attorney/startup services ($2,500+). Nobody owns the middle with a branded, tech-enabled experience.

2. **State-Specific Guidance:** Every state has different dissolution requirements, fees, and timelines. No single resource consolidates this comprehensively in an accessible format. This is high-value content that drives organic search traffic.

3. **The "Zombie LLC" Problem:** Hundreds of thousands of business owners are paying annual state fees on entities they think are closed. An awareness + dissolution service targeting this segment is wide open.

4. **The CPA/Attorney Channel (B2B):** Professionals handling dissolutions for clients have no streamlined SaaS tool. They manually navigate state websites for each client.

5. **Multi-State Dissolution:** Businesses registered in multiple states face compounding complexity. A service that coordinates all state filings (home state dissolution + foreign state withdrawals) commands premium pricing.

6. **Post-Dissolution Compliance:** Making sure final tax returns are filed, EINs are closed, state tax accounts are closed, business licenses are canceled -- the "last mile" that filing services ignore.

### Timing Window

The timing is exceptionally favorable:

- **2020-2021 formation boom fallout:** ~10 million business applications filed in 2020-2021. With a 20% Year 1 failure rate and 50% Year 5 failure rate, the dissolution wave is arriving NOW (2025-2026) and will intensify through 2028.
- **AI readiness:** AI tools can now automate state-specific guidance, document preparation, and compliance checking at a fraction of the cost of human labor.
- **Competitor gaps:** ZenBusiness and Northwest -- two of the top 5 formation services -- do not offer dissolution. Their millions of customers will need dissolution services eventually.
- **Rising state compliance enforcement:** States are increasingly penalizing "zombie" entities with fees and administrative dissolution, creating urgency.

### Recommended Entry Strategy

**Phase 1: Content + Information Product (Months 1-3)**
- Build a comprehensive state-by-state dissolution guide (all 50 states)
- Monetize at $49-$79 as a digital product / interactive tool
- Aggressive SEO targeting "how to dissolve LLC in [state]" (50 landing pages)
- This builds authority, captures email leads, and generates immediate revenue
- **Revenue target:** $5K-$20K/month

**Phase 2: Guided Dissolution Service (Months 3-6)**
- Launch a $199-$499 "Guided Dissolution" tier (filing + compliance checklist + email support)
- Launch a $999 "Complete Dissolution" tier (full-service for simple businesses)
- Upsell Phase 1 guide buyers into service tiers
- **Revenue target:** $20K-$75K/month

**Phase 3: Multi-State + B2B (Months 6-12)**
- Add multi-state dissolution coordination ($1,500-$3,000)
- Build a CPA/attorney portal for professionals handling client dissolutions
- Consider a "Zombie LLC Finder" tool (search state databases, alert owners)
- Explore partnership/white-label with ZenBusiness or Northwest (who lack this product)
- **Revenue target:** $75K-$250K/month

## 8. BOTTOM LINE

**The business dissolution market is a $500M+ annual opportunity with a structural demand surge arriving now.** The 2020-2021 entrepreneurship boom created ~10 million new businesses, half of which will eventually need dissolution services. The market currently splits between commodity filing services ($99-$149) and expensive professional services ($2,500+), leaving a wide-open middle market.

The strongest entry point is a **content-led strategy** (state-by-state dissolution guides for SEO) that funnels into a **tiered service model** ($49 guide / $199-$499 guided / $999 full-service). The absence of dissolution products from ZenBusiness and Northwest creates both a competitive opening and a potential partnership/acquisition opportunity.

**The window is now.** The pandemic-cohort closure wave is arriving, AI makes guided legal services cheaper to deliver than ever, and no player owns the "dissolution" brand the way LegalZoom owns "formation."

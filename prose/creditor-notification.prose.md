# Creditor Notification Workflow

## Purpose
Guide a user through the creditor notification process during business dissolution.

## When to Use
During wind-down phase, before filing dissolution papers.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Identify All Known Creditors
1. Review accounts payable
2. Review outstanding loans and lines of credit
3. Review vendor contracts with outstanding obligations
4. Review leases (real estate, equipment)
5. Review pending lawsuits or claims
6. Review employee-related obligations (wages, benefits, severance)
7. Review tax obligations

### Step 2: Send Written Notice to Known Creditors
For each known creditor, send a letter including:
- Name and address of the dissolving business
- Statement that the business is dissolving
- Deadline for filing claims (per state statute, typically 90-120 days)
- Address where claims should be sent
- Statement that claims not filed by the deadline may be barred

### Step 3: Publication Notice (if required by state)
Some states require publication in a newspaper. Check `state.requirements.publicationRequired`.
If required:
1. Draft notice of dissolution
2. Publish in newspaper of general circulation in county of principal office
3. Publish for required duration (varies by state)
4. Obtain affidavit of publication from newspaper

### Step 4: Wait for Claims Period
- Most states: 90-120 day claims period after notice
- Do not distribute remaining assets until claims period expires
- Process any claims received during the period

### Step 5: Process Claims
1. Review each claim received
2. Accept valid claims and add to payoff schedule
3. Reject invalid claims in writing with reason
4. Disputed claims may need legal resolution

### Step 6: Pay Claims
Pay in order of priority (general order):
1. Secured creditors (per security agreement)
2. Tax obligations (federal, state, local)
3. Employee wages and benefits
4. Unsecured creditors (pro rata if insufficient assets)

### Step 7: Document Everything
- Keep copies of all notices sent
- Keep proof of publication (if applicable)
- Keep all claims received and responses
- Keep proof of all payments
- Retain records for at least 7 years

## Letter Template

```
[Business Name]
[Business Address]
[Date]

[Creditor Name]
[Creditor Address]

RE: Notice of Dissolution - [Business Name]

Dear [Creditor Name],

This letter serves as formal notice that [Business Name], a [entity type] organized under the laws of [State], is being dissolved.

Pursuant to [State statute], you are hereby notified to present any claims you may have against [Business Name] in writing. Claims must be received no later than [Date - per state statute] at the following address:

[Claims Address]

Claims should include:
- Your name and address
- Description of the claim
- Amount claimed
- Supporting documentation

Claims not received by the deadline above may be barred pursuant to state law.

Sincerely,
[Authorized Person Name]
[Title]
[Business Name]
```
